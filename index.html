About Me



I grew up in the incredible, often grey and beyond uncomfortably-cold, crime-ridden city known as Chicago. The food scene and summertime festivities is what makes it all more than worthwhile. However, with a core temperature comparable to a lizard, Chicago winters were especially brutal. That's why, when the opportunity to move to Austin presented itself, I jumped on it. Of course, this meant, at the age of 26, I finally needed to learn how to drive.


I've spent most of my working career in leadership roles at food-related environments. From managing the cafeteria at the second oldest law school in Chicago, to holding a management role at a Chicago founded and based food distribution company with annual net sales of $50M. Since moving to Austin, I served a short stint as an Administrative Assistant at a Residential and Commercial Restoration company. This position included filing insurance claims and required an IICRC certification in Water Damage Restoration, in addition to the corporation's multiple internal certifications. For the past 5 years I've put those driving skills to use, commuting 40 miles roundtrip, to serve as the Administrative Assistant & Application Processor for the Food & Nutrition Services Department at a public school district in the Hill Country. Least we forget, I entered the district in the roll of a bouffant hairnet wearing lunch lady for a few months before quickly realizing I prefer standing desks to standing on my feet all day and that I have little to no rapport with elementary-aged children.
